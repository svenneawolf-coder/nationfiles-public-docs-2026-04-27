# The Global Re-Evaluation Framework

## Introduction
The **Global Re-Evaluation Framework** is the operational core of the [Naciro Engine](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139553602). It defines the methodology by which the world is re-interpreted every 24 hours. By shifting from static geopolitical snapshots to a continuous, high-frequency analytical cycle, this framework allows [NationFiles](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139473767) to provide a real-time "Predictive Layer" for global stability.

---

## Chapter 1: The 24-Hour Cycle of World Re-Interpretation
Geopolitics is not a static state but a fluid process. The Global Re-Evaluation Framework ensures that no data point older than 24 hours remains un-evaluated in the context of the current global situation.

### 1.1 Temporal Resolution
The cycle begins at 00:00 UTC and concludes with a full system update across all 195 nations. This 24-hour temporal resolution is designed to:
* **Neutralize Recency Bias:** By resetting the analytical baseline daily.
* **Identify Emerging Trends:** Detecting shifts in news sentiment and kinetic activity before they manifest as long-term crises.
* **Synchronize Global Data:** Ensuring that Forex volatility, conflict reports, and social sentiment are evaluated within the same temporal window.

---

## Chapter 2: The Three-Layer Ingestion & Analysis
The framework follows the [Technical Layer 1-3 Infrastructure](https://nationfiles.com/en/legal/layer1-3/) to ensure a structured flow of information.

### 2.1 Layer 1: Global Signal Harvesting
During each 24-hour cycle, the engine ingests millions of signals from the [NationFiles Source Directory](https://nationfiles.com/en/legal/sources/). This includes:
* **Event Data:** Real-time conflict logs (ACLED/UCDP).
* **Narrative Data:** Multi-language media monitoring (GDELT).
* **Economic Data:** Live macroeconomic shifts and currency fluctuations.

### 2.2 Layer 2: Neural Re-Processing
In this stage, the [Naciro Engine](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139553602) utilizes its LPU infrastructure to perform a full recalculation of all national stability scores. Unlike traditional systems that update only "active" regions, the Global Re-Evaluation processes every country, ensuring that secondary and tertiary effects of a crisis are identified globally.

---

## Chapter 3: Stability Recalculation (NFSI)
The outcome of the 24-hour cycle is the updated [NationFiles Stability Index (NFSI)](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139553766).

### 3.1 Dynamic Weighting
The framework applies a dynamic weighting system:
* **Standard Mode:** Macroeconomic anchors provide 60% of the stability score.
* **Alert Mode:** During high-volatility events, real-time OSINT signals can account for up to 80% of the score to reflect immediate risks.

### 3.2 Causality Simulation
The re-evaluation simulates potential "cascading failures." For example, the framework analyzes how a drop in stability in one nation affects the economic and political "vibe" of its neighboring regions within the next 24-hour window.

---

## Chapter 4: Output and Verification
Each daily cycle concludes with the generation of over 500,000 indexed pages in 7 languages (including JA, AR, and DE).

### 4.1 Automated Transparency
To maintain scientific credibility, every re-evaluation is documented according to the [Validation and Verification Report (VVR)](https://nationfiles.com/en/legal/validation-and-verification-report/). This ensures that the logic behind a stability shift is transparent and auditable by human analysts.

### 4.2 Algorithmic Governance
The entire 24-hour cycle is governed by the [NationFiles Governance Protocol](https://nationfiles.com/en/legal/governance/), preventing political interference and ensuring that [Sven Schmidt's](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139553554) architectural vision for a neutral, AI-driven world-view is maintained by the [Neawolf Media Group](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q139474781).
